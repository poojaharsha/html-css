Athletes at the 2014 Sochi Winter Olympics Game

Bjørn Dæhlie

In the 1990s, Norway’s Bjørn Dæhlie established himself as the most successful cross-country skier in history, notching up 29 Olympic and world championship podiums, including eight golds at the Winter Games and seven world titles.

Additionally, he scored a total of 46 victories in the FIS World Cup and won no less than six large crystal globes.

Ole Einar Bjoerndalen

Fact 1: Out of the fog and into the record books, Norway's Ole Einar Bjoerndalen became the most decorated Winter Olympian of all time after winning his 13th medal.Bjoerndalen had matched Daehlie's record when he won the men's sprint on the opening first Saturday of the Games. He could win a 14th medal in the men's relay on Saturday.

Fact 2: Fog and overcast weather at the Laura venue where the cross-country and biathlon events were taking place had caused a string of races to be rearranged. But that did not stop the Norwegian winning his 13th medal and his eighth gold, in the biathlon mixed relay.

Fact 3: He began his Olympic career at a home Games at Lillehammer in 1994 but won his first medals four years later in Nagano.

Fact4: Although his results are far from his usual standards, he does not seem to wear down and still has the hunger of a young wolf
